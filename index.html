Total Addressable Market (TAM) Calculations

The total annual revenue opportunity if every relevant building in Portugal fully adopted Nuuka AI's optimization. This includes all large commercial, services, and public buildings that could benefit from HVAC optimization, regardless of existing BMS presence or current willingness to invest.

Target Segment (TAM):

Large commercial, public administration, hospitality, retail, and institutional buildings that significantly consume energy for HVAC.

Estimating Building Count:

Estimating the number of "large commercial/public" buildings is complex. A reasonable assumption for buildings large enough to benefit significantly from Nuuka AI's enterprise-level solution (typically with existing, complex HVAC systems) might be 50,000 to 100,000 buildings. This is a broad estimate for all buildings where an advanced HVAC optimization could be implemented.

Average Annual HVAC Energy Cost per Building (Estimate):

Assuming an average size of 2,000 m² for a target building.

Total Annual Energy Consumption: 2,000m² × 94.4kWh/m² = 188,800kWh

HVAC Energy Consumption: 188,800kWh × 34% = 64,192kWh

Annual HVAC Energy Cost: 64,192kWh × €0.22/kWh = €14,122.24

Potential Annual Savings per Building (Nuuka AI):

€14,122.24 × 15% = €2,118.34

Total Addressable Market (TAM):

100,000 buildings × €2,118.34/building = €211,834,000 per year in potential savings.

This represents the total value of potential savings that Nuuka AI could unlock. If Nuuka charges a percentage of these savings (e.g., 50%), then the TAM for Nuuka's revenue would be approximately €105 Million annually.

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM) Calculations

The portion of TAM that Nuuka AI can realistically serve, considering its specific product (AI for existing BMS HVAC) and business model (zero CAPEX, subscription).

SAM Filters:

Buildings must have:

  • An existing Building Management System (BMS).
  • Significant HVAC energy consumption (typically larger commercial/public buildings).
  • Willingness to adopt a SaaS model for energy efficiency.

Prevalence of BMS:

While precise data for Portugal is scarce, industry reports suggest that a substantial portion of large commercial and public buildings, particularly newer or modernized ones, have some form of BMS. Conservatively, let's estimate that 20-30% of the TAM buildings would have an existing BMS sophisticated enough for Nuuka AI integration. We'll use 25%.

Number of SAM Buildings:

100,000 buildings (TAM) × 25% = 25,000 buildings

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):

25,000 buildings × €2,118.34/building (potential savings) = €52,958,500 per year in potential savings.

Nuuka AI's revenue SAM (assuming 50% of savings): €26.5 Million annually.
